Who's Megha?

Megha Bhouraskar has an international reputation and a client-base spanning continents. While she grew up in New York City and attended Columbia University/Barnard College and the United Nations International School, her childhood summers were spent draped in crisp cotton sarees, with her grandparents in Gwalior and Indore, and visiting friends in Mumbai and Delhi. This background has added several dimensions to her perspective on both personal and professional fronts. Megha continues to travel regularly to India and other parts of the World while she lives and practices in New York City.

Megha’s foray into law, was initially, for a ‘ticket to earn’ while she wrote fiction and poetry. Then, her passion for innovative viewpoints and strategizing for clients, writing and speaking practice, paired with her empathetic coaching, negotiating and transactional skills, set her apart as one of New York’s most trusted international names in law and business.

PROVIDING PEACE OF MIND

Most of her clients say working with Megha is a comforting experience because she allows them to freely express themselves and to focus on what they do best while she takes care of the rest. Her knowledge, passion, loyalty, empathy and reassuring disposition passes on to the most frantic of folks.

Coaching

From entrepreneurship to solving business issues, Megha is a life and entrepreneurial coach to many. This has both become Megha’s expertise and area of interest. Megha offers an experienced and knowledgeable ear and offers solutions and strategies for the toughest of situations for her clients.

Reach out for a consultation, you’ll be glad you did.

giving back

When Megha’s managed to squeeze time from advocating successfully for her clients, she’s found volunteering her skills for non profit organizations such as, Board & Charter Member of TiE (The Indus Entrepreneur) NY, TiE Women (Mentor for Project “AIRSWEE”-Mentoring Women Entrepreneurs in India in Phase 2 Development), and TiE Global, and Founder & Former Board Member of Sakhi for South Asian Women. What she enjoys most about projects such as these, is the connections she builds and ideas and experiences exchanged on a human level with people who’re also passionate about entrepreneurship.
